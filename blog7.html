Course Reflection

By Juan Moreno

During final presentations I learnt that: time, team, and communication management is really essential to the development of a game. I must say for my game my a certain group member fell short and almost never met when our group was working on the game. I felt like he almost did not contribute, but that was partly due to us the programmers having issues and not being able to expand the game more. But from what I saw on other people’s presentations, although they did not mention it, you could tell if the members were working as a team or not. It sure was nice hearing feedback on how we and everyone else could improve, from people during presentations and outside the classroom.

The material covered in class was very related to the crafting of a game and how to properly do it, along with other elements that come with them: such as how to properly implement mechanics, play test, dynamics and aesthetics. The professor did a great job at providing enough material to gain knowledge on these subjects. And although people might’ve not shown mastery of these in their game, I’m pretty sure by now they have those concepts etched in their minds thanks to this class.

Overall, based on what I saw, It sure would’ve been nice to have some time to work on the game during class, that way we could ask questions from other classmates, the instructor and on top of that have the whole group in sync. I personally believe that doing that would really benefit future game designers in Art/CS 108.
